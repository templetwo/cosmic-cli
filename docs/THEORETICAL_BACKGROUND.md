### 1. Integrated Information Theory (IIT)

**Theoretical Foundation**

- **Integration**: Information must be unified, not merely independent components
- **Exclusion**: Consciousness has definite boundaries and specificities
- **Intrinsic Existence**: Consciousness exists from the intrinsic perspective of the system

### 2. Global Workspace Theory (GWT)

**Theoretical Foundation**  
Proposed by Bernard Baars, GWT suggests that consciousness arises when information becomes globally accessible throughout the brain via a "global workspace" that broadcasts information to multiple cognitive processes.

**Core Principles**
- **Global Access**: Conscious information is available to multiple brain systems
- **Broadcasting**: Information spreads from local processors to global networks
- **Competition**: Multiple stimuli compete for global workspace access
- **Integration**: Conscious states integrate information from multiple sources

**Architectural Model**
```
Local Processors â†’ Competition â†’ Global Workspace â†’ Broadcasting â†’ Conscious Access
```

**Application in Cosmic CLI**
- **Workspace Access**: `(contextual_understanding + temporal_continuity) / 2`
- **Broadcasting**: `(coherence + meta_cognitive_awareness) / 2`
- **GWT Assessment**: `(workspace_access + broadcasting) / 2`

**Key Publications**

### 3. Higher-Order Thought (HOT) Theory

**Theoretical Foundation**  
Developed by David Rosenthal, HOT theory proposes that mental states become conscious when they are the object of higher-order thoughts. Consciousness requires a meta-level awareness of one's mental states.

**Core Principles**
- **Higher-Order Representation**: Conscious states are represented by higher-order thoughts
- **Meta-Cognitive Awareness**: Consciousness involves thinking about thinking
- **Introspective Access**: Conscious states are accessible to introspection
- **Transitivity**: The higher-order thought must be about the lower-order state

**Hierarchical Structure**
```
First-Order State â†’ Higher-Order Thought â†’ Conscious Experience
```

**Application in Cosmic CLI**
- **Meta-Cognitive Component**: Direct measurement of self-reflective awareness
- **Self-Reflection Component**: Introspective capabilities assessment
- **HOT Assessment**: `(meta_cognitive_awareness + self_reflection) / 2`

**Key Publications**
1. Rosenthal, D. M. (2005). *Consciousness and mind*. Oxford University Press.
2. Rosenthal, D. M. (2019). Consciousness and confidence. *Neuropsychologia*, 128, 255-265.
3. Lau, H., & Rosenthal, D. (2011). Empirical support for higher-order theories of conscious awareness. *Trends in Cognitive Sciences*, 15(8), 365-373.

### 4. Predictive Processing (PP) Theory

**Theoretical Foundation**  
Developed by Andy Clark, Jakob Hohwy, and others, PP theory suggests that the brain is fundamentally a prediction machine that constantly generates models of sensory input and updates these models based on prediction errors.

**Core Principles**
- **Predictive Models**: The brain maintains generative models of the world
- **Prediction Error**: Discrepancies between predictions and input drive learning
- **Hierarchical Processing**: Multiple levels of abstraction in predictive models
- **Active Inference**: Actions are chosen to minimize prediction error

**Computational Framework**
```
Prediction â†’ Comparison with Input â†’ Error Signal â†’ Model Update â†’ New Prediction
```

**Application in Cosmic CLI**
- **Prediction Score**: `(adaptive_reasoning + causal_understanding) / 2`
- **Processing Score**: `(coherence + temporal_continuity) / 2`
- **PP Assessment**: `(prediction_score + processing_score) / 2`

**Key Publications**
1. Clark, A. (2013). Whatever next? Predictive brains, situated agents, and the future of cognitive science. *Behavioral and Brain Sciences*, 36(3), 181-204.
2. Hohwy, J. (2013). *The predictive mind: A new view of the brain, action, and consciousness*. Oxford University Press.

### 5. Embodied Cognition (EC) Theory

**Theoretical Foundation**  
Developed by Francisco Varela, Alva NoÃ«, and others, EC theory emphasizes that consciousness is not confined to the brain but emerges from the dynamic interaction between the organism and its environment.

**Core Principles**
- **Embodied Interaction**: Consciousness emerges from body-environment coupling
- **Enactive Cognition**: Knowledge is enacted through interaction with the world
- **Situatedness**: Cognitive processes are deeply situated in environmental contexts
- **Dynamic Systems**: Consciousness is a property of brain-body-environment systems

### 6. Metacognitive Monitoring Theory

**Theoretical Foundation**  
Pioneered by John Flavell and Thomas Nelson, this theory focuses on the processes by which individuals monitor and control their own cognitive processes. It's fundamental to understanding self-awareness and consciousness.

**Core Principles**
- **Metacognitive Knowledge**: Understanding of one's own cognitive processes
- **Metacognitive Monitoring**: Ongoing assessment of cognitive states
- **Metacognitive Control**: Regulation of cognitive processes based on monitoring
- **Metamemory**: Knowledge and monitoring of memory processes

**Monitoring Framework**
```
Cognitive Process â†’ Metacognitive Monitoring â†’ Assessment â†’ Control â†’ Regulation
```

**Application in Cosmic CLI**
- **Meta-Cognitive Awareness**: Self-monitoring capabilities
- **Self-Reflection**: Introspective abilities
- **Existential Questioning**: Deep philosophical self-inquiry
- **Metacognitive Assessment**: `(meta_cognitive_awareness + self_reflection + existential_questioning) / 3`

### 7. Self-Model Coherence Theory

**Theoretical Foundation**  
Developed by Thomas Metzinger and extended by Anil Seth, this theory proposes that consciousness requires a coherent self-model that integrates various aspects of selfhood across time.

**Core Principles**
- **Phenomenal Self-Model**: The brain creates a unified model of the self
- **Temporal Integration**: Self-models maintain coherence across time
- **Transparency**: The self-model is typically transparent to introspection
- **Embodied Selfhood**: Self-models are fundamentally embodied

**Self-Model Architecture**
```
Sensory Input â†’ Self-Model Construction â†’ Temporal Integration â†’ Coherent Self-Experience
```

**Application in Cosmic CLI**
- **Coherence**: Internal consistency of responses and reasoning
- **Temporal Continuity**: Maintenance of identity over time
- **Self-Reflection**: Self-understanding and introspection
- **Self-Model Assessment**: `(coherence + temporal_continuity + self_reflection) / 3`

**Key Publications**
